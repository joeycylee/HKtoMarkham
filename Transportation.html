Transportation

Driving in the winter:

  • Wake up earlier to shovel snow, warm up the car (turn it on for 2-3 minutes before driving), and scrape the ice off the windshield.
    • If you are warming the car in the garage, open the garage door. If the garage door is closed, you can die from carbon monoxide poisoning.
    • Lock the car doors when warming the car. Some cars have been stolen because people left their unlocked car on the driveway.
  • Before a snow storm, put some salt on your porch and driveway so it does not get too slippery in the morning.
  • Don't drive too close to the car in front of you. Ice OR snow can cause your car to slip, especially if you press the brake very hard.
  • If your car is new, the tires are probably good enough for its first winter. Starting next winter (the car's second winter), get winter tires.
    • Most people store their tires in the garage.
    • Insurance companies prefer that you use winter tires during the winter. This can lower your insurance costs. But winter tires cost a lot. Car dealers say that normal tires are good for the first winter because the tires still have good grip. It's up to you!
  • Get good winter boots so you don't slip. This website tells you how good your boots are.

Click here for more tips on surviving Canadian winters.

Other important car tips:

  • Protecting your car from rust ever year can lower the amount you pay to insurance companies.
  • You must renew your license plate online every 1 to 2 years. The government does not remind you. You can create a reminder on their website here.
  • When you are in a parking lot, lock your car as soon as you get in so strangers can't follow you inside.

How to Pay

You can use your debit card, credit card, or Presto card to pay.

A Presto card lets you use many transportation companies in the Toronto area.

  • To pay for a bus ride by YRT (York Region) or TTC (Toronto), tap your Presto card on the machine ON the bus.
  • To pay for a bus ride by VIVA (York Region), tap your Presto card on the machine at the BUS STATION (not ON the bus) before you get on the bus.
  • To pay for a GO train or GO bus ride, tap your Presto card BEFORE you go on the train AND AFTER you get off the train.

You can add money to your Presto card on their website, on their app, or in person (at most subway stations).

Bus times on Google Maps are usually accurate. Or you can use Triplinx.
