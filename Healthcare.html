Healthcare

Click here to see a full list of what is covered by the government (OHIP).

Everyone in Ontario is covered by the Ontario Health Insurance Plan (OHIP). Some jobs make you pay for a private health insurance plan (they take money out of your pay cheque to pay for this). These plans give you more health insurance. If you do not have a health insurance plan from your job, you can still get free healthcare from OHIP. OHIP lets you use these services for free:

  • Visits to a doctor (family doctor, walk-in clinic, specialist). If you need to see a specialist, you must ask your family doctor to refer you to the specialist first.
  • Hospital care
  • Diagnostic services: blood tests, x-rays, ultrasounds, etc.
  • Flu shots (flu vaccine): These are free at any pharmacy and you can get one every year. Flu season starts in October.

You must pay for health services that OHIP does NOT cover. These services are:

  • Prescription medications
    • If you need medications regularly, you can apply to get money from Trillium. You have to pay for the medication first and then Trillium will give you the money.
  • Eye exams
    • BUT! If you are 20 - 64 years old and you have an eye disease, OHIP covers 1 major eye exam every 12 months and 2 follow-up minor exams.
    • BUT! If you are 65+ years old and do not have eye disease, OHIP covers 1 major eye exam every 18 months and 2 follow-up minor exams.
  • Glasses, contact lenses
  • Laser eye surgery
  • The dentist

Children

  • If you are under 25 years old AND your parents do not have private health insurance, OHIP+ covers many prescription medications. To find out if your prescription medication is covered by OHIP+, click here.
  • If you are under 19 years old, OHIP covers 1 major eye exam every 12 months.

  • Normally, the family doctor will only discuss ONE topic with you (like back pain or sore throat, but not both). Start your appointment with your most important topic.
  • Write down what the doctor tells you: medications, explanations, disease names, etc.
